NBA Analysis and Web Crawler

Analysis of phenomena in the NBA

This project was my final project for my Data Programming class, which was in Python. The goal was to find a problem and find a dataset related to that problem to analyze and answer questions regarding this problem. The problem I decided to look at was to analyze certain phenomenon in the NBA, such as is the notion of a hot/cold streak a statistically quantifiable idea, also how the toll of an 82 game season can affect team performance.

So I first set out to obtain the dataset using a crawler, which was written in Python, but much more primitive compared to the one I created in my Everything Sports application. However, I do see it as a good learning experience, because up to that point I had no idea how to crawl a webpage. I think another challenge at that point was that I didn’t know HTML so didn’t understand the hierarchy of pages and instead just had to do it blindly. The result was that the data that it obtained was highly unnormalized, so I had to find an alternative data source.

I ended up finding a publicly available dataset of various NBA statistics, including play by play and individual player statistics. I had to first take the data and format it, so I had to learn how to traverse file directories. Once I got to the data files, I read from it and placed that data into a dictionary, one for play-by-play, and another for player statistics.

Once the data was formatted, I used it in various ways, such as analyzing shot locations, and creates a scatterplot as well as a heat map of those shot locations for each season. Not only this, but I also examined the notion of a cold/hot streak, to see if it was a true phenomenon with data to back it. Finally I also looked at team’s turnover rate before and after the halfway point in the season (all-star break).

Although the methodology may not have been perfect, it was a good learning experience on how to handle and analyze large amount of data. I learned a lot of different things, including using matplotlib, pylab, and numpy to analyze large amount of information.
